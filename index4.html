RASCAL Competition Team Lead

For the NASA RASCAL proposal writing competition, our team was tasked with designing a large-scale south pole lunar crater prospector. We approached this theme with a multi-system design consisting of specialized components connected largely by a system of tethers. This system included a base at the crater rim for power generation, twin rovers for prospecting the crater housed within a module for travel into the crater, and a compartment to travel between the base and rim for sample transport.

As the team lead, I created a detailed gantt chart to set internal deadlines and break down tasks. I also made sure to include buffers in the schedule, encourage constant communication, and set clear goals for each meeting. To build a strong team culture, I focused on delegating tasks, increasing member ownership, scheduling team bonding, and recognizing achievments.

Much of the early stages were occupied by extensive research into existing and upcoming technologies, lunar environments, and NASA’s objectives. Once a design was finalized, I focused on creating detailed mass, power, and monetary budgets, and modeling the designs on CAD. I specifically focused on designing the prospecting rover, and developing a clear CONOPS for the mission. I also worked on a video simulating our operations as well as a mission patch to represent our design. By the end of the year, the team had produced a written proposal and short video for our mission concept.
