Engineering Inspired Art

Visual storytelling and outreach work connecting space exploration, design, and creativity.

I enjoy exploring the intersection between engineering and art as a way to make space missions more engaging and accessible. As a Psyche Inspired Intern , I created four original pieces for NASA’s Psyche Mission outreach, each reviewed for both scientific accuracy and visual impact.

The collection included a chalkboard-style marker drawing highlighting the mission’s underlying physics, an acrylic painting imagining everyday life aboard the spacecraft, and an experimental colored pencil piece on wood inspired by Psyche’s metallic composition.

I also designed a Psyche-themed card game as an educational outreach concept, which the mission PI requested additional copies of for her team.

Beyond Psyche, I apply creative design skills across student aerospace projects including Columbia Space Initiative mission t-shirts representing all 13 missions, and the mission patch for the 6U CubeSat LIONESS, developed collaboratively with team feedback.
