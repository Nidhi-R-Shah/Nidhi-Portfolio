CubeSat Mission Control Interface Design

Human-centered command and telemetry interface designed through iterative operator testing.

For Aerospace Human Factors Engineering, I worked on a team of four to design a mission control interface for monitoring and commanding a CubeSat spacecraft. The display was developed as a general ground control prototype, with planned adaptation for Columbia’s upcoming CubeSat missions.

We iterated through multiple interface concepts and control schemes, including touchscreen interaction, keyboard-based commanding, and structured pseudocode inputs, based on feedback from CubeSat team members as our target user group.

Using a modified Cooper-Harper evaluation scale, we assessed workload, usability, and operator confidence across display layouts (integrated vs. separated telemetry views) to converge on an effective command-and-monitoring workflow.

The final interface visualizes spacecraft position, attitude, thermal state, and telemetry trends, while providing a configurable command window for real-time mission operations.

You can view our final project paper and presentation for additional design rationale and testing results.
