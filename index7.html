JPL Internship ATLO Tooling

As a Mechanical Engineering intern at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, I worked on the Separations team for the proposed Mars Sample Return mission, focusing on hardware used during ATLO (Assembly, Test, and Launch Operations).

Press-Fit Tool Redesign: Designed a compact, modular tool able to apply ~5000 lbf to install the spacecraft's cup-and-cone separation interfaces. Coordinated with ATLO, Contamination Control, and Planetary Protection to ensure the tool was safe and practical for use near flight hardware.

Torque Multiplier GSE: Developed ground support equipment to interface a torque multiplier with the Separation Nut, ensuring the design was accessible within tight clearances in the spacecraft structure.

Mass Model Sizing Tool: Created an Excel tool linked to CAD to automatically compute mass-model geometry for electrical boxes used in the Developmental Test Model (DTM) for structural and vibration testing.
